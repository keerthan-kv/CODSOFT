The Miura was known for its impressive performance, with a top speed of over 170 mph (273 km/h) and a 0-60 mph (0-97 km/h) time of around 6 seconds.
The chassis is a tubular steel frame that provides structural support to the car. This design choice contributes to the Miura's lightweight and agile characteristics.
The interior boasted a mix of comfort and performance, with features such as a wood-rimmed steering wheel, classic gauges, and a center console that housed various controls.
The Miura typically had a quad-exhaust system, with two exhaust pipes exiting on each side of the car. The sound of the Miura's V12 engine combined with its exhaust system is often praised for its distinct and thrilling tone.
