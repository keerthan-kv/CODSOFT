The production numbers varied for each version, and a limited number of road-going GT40s were also produced for homologation purposes. Approximately 87 GT40 Mk I cars were produced.
The chassis of the GT40 was a monocoque design, utilizing a lightweight aluminum structure. The chassis design evolved through different versions to improve aerodynamics, handling, and overall performance.
TInitially, the GT40 was powered by a Ford 4.2-liter V8 engine. As the program evolved, the engine size increased, and the GT40 became synonymous with the 7.0-liter (427 cubic inches) V8 engine.
It featured bucket seats, a minimalist dashboard with essential gauges, and a steering wheel designed for optimal control. Road-going versions had slightly more comfort-oriented interiors compared to their racing counterparts.
